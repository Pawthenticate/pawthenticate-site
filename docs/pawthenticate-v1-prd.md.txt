# Pawthenticate – V1 MVP PRD

**Product name:** Pawthenticate  
**Tagline:** *Where your pet’s story lives*  
**Version:** V1 (MVP)  
**Market:** Australia  
**Platform:** Mobile-first web app

---

## 1. Product Overview

### 1.1 One-sentence description

Pawthenticate is a simple web app that helps Australian renters quickly turn their pet’s information into a clean, landlord-ready “pet resume” they can print or send with almost zero effort or confusion.

### 1.2 Problem

#### For renters with pets

- They don’t know what landlords/property managers actually want to see – temperament, vet info, references, photos, etc.
- They hack something together in Word/Canva/Notes, fight with formatting, image layout, and fonts, and often end up with something that looks unprofessional.
- Every new rental application means finding old files, tweaking details, re-exporting PDFs, and renaming messes like `PetResume_Final_REALfinal2.pdf`.

#### For property managers & landlords

- Every applicant sends pet info in a different format: random PDFs, long emails, screenshots, etc.
- It’s hard to quickly find the key risk items: desexed, vaccinated, behaviour, references, living situation.
- They often need back-and-forth with tenants to get missing documents, clearer photos, or confirmation of basic details.

### 1.3 Solution

Pawthenticate provides:

- A guided form that already “knows” what Aussie property managers care about.
- A standardised, professional PDF that always looks good and is easy to skim.
- A mobile-first experience so renters can do this on their phone in a few minutes instead of wrestling with Word or Canva.

### 1.4 Primary Goal (V1 / MVP)

> Let a renter quickly turn basic info about their pet into a clear, landlord-ready “pet resume” they can print or send with almost zero effort or confusion.

## 2. Target Users

### 2.1 Primary user

- Australian renters with pets who need to prove to landlords/property managers that their animal is responsible and well cared for.

### 2.2 Secondary users

- Property managers and landlords who read Pawthenticate pet resumes when screening applications.

---

## 3. Value Proposition

### 3.1 For renters

- **No guesswork:** The form guides them through what to include.
- **No formatting pain:** They never touch Word/Canva – they just fill in fields and get a polished PDF.
- **Quick to repeat:** They can revisit the form on the same device and update details instead of recreating from scratch.

### 3.2 For property managers / landlords

- **Faster screening:** The same structure every time; key facts (desexed, vaccinated, behaviour, documents) are all in predictable places.
- **More confidence:** Temperament notes, health info, and clear details present the pet as a low-risk, well-managed companion.
- **Less chasing tenants:** A single tidy resume reduces follow-up about “can you send their vaccine certificate” or “do you have a better photo”.

### 3.3 Why Pawthenticate vs Canva/Word

> Pawthenticate already knows what Aussie property managers want to see and turns your pet’s info into a polished, standardised resume in minutes, with zero formatting headaches.

---

## 4. Scope – V1 MVP

**Concept:** Single pet, all core pet info + required docs → one clean, rental-focused PDF.  
No login, no backend accounts. Local-first.

### 4.1 In-scope (V1)

- Single pet resume per browser/device.
- Guided form with core pet info.
- Upload of key documents (vaccination, desexing, microchip paperwork).
- Preview screen that matches final PDF layout.
- Single clean PDF template, rental-focused.
- PDF output via browser print dialog (Download / Print).
- Mobile-first responsive design.

### 4.2 Out-of-scope (V1)

- Multiple pets management.
- Owner/household details beyond pet-specific info.
- Pet sitting/boarding mode.
- Shareable public links.
- Logins or cloud storage.
- Combined “household with multiple pets” PDF.
- Template/theme chooser.

---

## 5. Core User Flow (V1)

1. **Home page**
   - Short explanation of what Pawthenticate does.
   - Primary CTA: **“Create Pet Resume”**.

2. **Create Pet Resume – Form**
   - Sections:
     1. Pet basics  
     2. Identification & legal  
     3. Health, safety & insurance  
     4. Behaviour & living situation  
     5. Required uploads (documents)
   - Button: **“Save & Preview”**.

3. **Preview**
   - Shows the resume as it will appear in the PDF:
     - Clean header with pet photo & basics.
     - Sections laid out clearly (health, behaviour, documents).
   - Controls:
     - **“Back to Edit”**
     - **“Print / Save as PDF”**

4. **Export**
   - Clicking **“Print / Save as PDF”** opens the browser print dialog.
   - User can:
     - Print directly.
     - Save as PDF (standard browser option).

## 6. Data Model & Fields (V1)

### 6.1 Pet basics

- `petName` – text, required  
- `species` – select: Dog / Cat / Other, required  
- `breed` – text, optional but recommended  
- `ageOrDOB` – either an age field or date of birth (implementation choice), required  
- `sizeDescription` – text (e.g. “Small, 6kg”), recommended  
- `photo` – single image upload (JPG/PNG), required  

### 6.2 Identification & legal

- `microchipNumber` – text, required  
- `councilRegistrationNumber` – text, optional  

### 6.3 Health, safety & insurance

- `desexed` – boolean (Yes/No), required  
- `vaccinationsUpToDate` – boolean (Yes/No), required  
- `lastFleaWormTreatmentDate` – date or month/year, optional  
- `hasPetInsurance` – boolean (Yes/No), optional  
- `petInsuranceProvider` – text, required if `hasPetInsurance = Yes`

### 6.4 Behaviour & living situation

- `temperamentSummary` – short text area (e.g. 2–3 sentences), required  
- `livingLocation` – select: Indoors / Outdoors / Mix, required  
- `goodWith` – multi-select / checkboxes:
  - Kids
  - Babies
  - Dogs
  - Cats
  - Elderly
  - Tradespeople
- `noiseLevel` – simple label, e.g.:
  - Low
  - Medium
  - High
- `houseTrainingStatus` – select:
  - Fully house-trained
  - Crate-trained
  - Still learning
  - Not yet trained

### 6.5 Required uploads (documents)

- `vaccinationCertificate` – file upload  
- `desexingCertificate` – file upload  
- `microchipPaperwork` – file upload  

> For V1, documents can be treated as attached/not attached and summarised on the PDF; embedding full document pages is not required.

## 7. PDF Output – V1 Template

V1 uses a **single, rental-focused template**.

### 7.1 Page 1 content (must-have)

**Header**

- Big pet name.  
- Species, breed, age, size (e.g. `Dog • Border Collie mix • 3 years • Small, 6kg`).  
- Clear pet photo.

**Key facts (at-a-glance “green tick” area)**

- Desexed (Yes/No).  
- Vaccinations up to date (Yes/No).  
- Microchip number.  
- Council registration number.  
- Pet insurance (Yes/No + provider name if Yes).

**Behaviour & living situation**

- Short temperament paragraph.  
- “Good with” row (icons/labels for Kids, Babies, Dogs, Cats, Elderly, Tradespeople).  
- Noise level (Low / Medium / High).  
- House training status.  
- Where the pet usually stays (Indoors / Outdoors / Mix).

**Documents summary**

- Small list with tick marks if documents are attached:
  - Vaccination certificate ✔/✗  
  - Desexing certificate ✔/✗  
  - Microchip paperwork ✔/✗  

**Footer**

- “Created with Pawthenticate” + URL (e.g. `pawthenticate.com`).

> V1 aims for a single-page PDF. If content grows later, a second page can be added in a future version.

## 8. Technical Approach (V1)

- **Platform:** Web app, mobile-first.
- **Suggested stack (non-binding):**
  - Next.js + React
  - TypeScript
  - Tailwind CSS
  - shadcn/ui components
- **Data handling:**
  - No login, no backend auth.
  - Store current pet data in `localStorage` so users don’t lose everything on refresh.
  - File uploads can be kept client-side only for MVP.

- **Export:**
  - Use browser print-to-PDF with dedicated print styles rather than server-side PDF generation.

## 9. Business & Pricing (V1)

### 9.1 MVP 1.0 (build & learning phase)

- Completely free.
- Small “Created with Pawthenticate” watermark on PDF.
- No login, no payments.
- Focus: validate that the workflow feels great and that renters/agents find it useful.

*A later 1.1+ version can introduce a free vs paid split; that’s covered in the roadmap doc, not required for V1 build.*

---

## 10. Success Metrics (V1)

- **Resumes created**
  - Target: 50–100 completed resumes by real users in the first month.

- **Completion rate**
  - At least 60–70% of users who start the form reach the PDF download step.

- **Real-world usage & feedback**
  - At least 10+ renters use Pawthenticate resumes in actual rental applications.
  - Qualitative feedback like:
    - “The agent commented on how clear it was.”
    - “They loved that everything was on one page.”

If these conditions are met, V1 is considered successful and ready for expansion.
